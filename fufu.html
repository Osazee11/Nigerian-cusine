Ingredient for yam

  • 1 Yam tuber
  • water

Ingredient for Egusi Soup

  • 1 cup Egusi (melon) seeds
  • Assorted meat (beef, chicken, or fish)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2-3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 2 cups chopped spinach or bitter leaf (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Ground crayfish (optional)
  • Seasoning cubes or powder

Prepare the yam:

  • Peel the yam and cut it into chunks.
  • Boil the yam in a pot of water until soft.
  • Drain the water and transfer the boiled yam to a mortar.
  • Pound the yam using a pestle until smooth and stretchy. If you don't have a mortar and pestle, you can use a stand mixer or food processor for this step. Prepare the Egusi Soup:
  • Grind the egusi seeds into a fine powder using a blender or food processor.
  • Heat the palm oil in a pot over medium heat.
  • Add the chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until softened.
  • Stir in the ground egusi and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  • Add the assorted meat and season with salt, seasoning cubes/powder, and ground crayfish (if using).
  • Pour in the vegetable broth or water and bring to a simmer.
  • Let the soup cook for about 20-30 minutes until the egusi is fully cooked and the flavors have melded together.
  • If using spinach or bitter leaf, add them to the soup during the last few minutes of cooking and stir until wilted.
  • Adjust the seasoning if necessary.

Serve:

  • Serve the pounded yam alongside the hot egusi soup.
  • Enjoy the delicious combination of flavors and textures!
